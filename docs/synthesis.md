# The Synthesis

A single decision-ready brief for the men's sexual wellness app.
Built by Saurabh Upadhyay. Mumbai, April 2026.

---

## 1. The thesis

> Treat me as the worst user. How best can we take him? If he follows diligently, he sees the result. Build the best of the best, as a solo dev, without spending a single penny.

That's the brief. Everything in this document serves that one sentence.

Two corollaries follow:

**Health is greater than performance.** Being healthy matters more than being good at sexual things. The app screens for medical red flags before it sells anything, refers users to doctors when their issue is organic, and never frames performance as the price of manhood.

**Agency is greater than shame.** The user has already been through enough. The app's job is to make him feel safe, then capable, then confident — in that order. Fear-driven copy, countdown timers, and forced paywalls are banned because they betray the user's trust at the moment he is most vulnerable.

## 2. The user, including the one nobody serves

The five competing AI dossiers identified eight personas. Your direct audit confirmed seven of them. But your own onboarding experience surfaced an eighth that no app — Mojo, Coral, Slow Down, Allo, Bold Care, Brainbuddy, QUITTR — currently serves. We will lead with it.

### Persona Zero: The Solo Trainee

The man who cannot honestly answer "during sex, how often are you stuck in your head?" because he has not had sex recently, or ever. Single after a long gap. Never had a partner. In a sexless marriage. Recovering from PIED through deliberate abstinence. Preparing for an arranged marriage. Religious or cultural abstinence pre-marriage.

Every competitor's intake assumes current sexual activity. Every competitor's progress metric assumes partnered feedback. Every competitor's content assumes a present-tense "you and your partner."

This persona is enormous in India. Conservatively 40% of men aged 22-32 in urban India fall in this bucket given marriage timing, dating culture, and religious abstinence norms. Globally it's smaller but still substantial — incels, recovering addicts, divorced men, widowers, men with social anxiety.

This persona needs:

- An intake that does not assume sexual experience
- A solo training path that builds capability without partner feedback
- Confidence-building rehearsal: mental, somatic, masturbatory
- Progress markers that don't require a sex life to measure
- A way to know "I am ready" before the first or next encounter

This is the wedge. We lead with it.

### The other personas, in priority order for MVP

1. **Persona Zero (solo trainee)** — covered above
2. **Lifelong PE** — finishes in under a minute, every time, has never lasted longer
3. **PIED (porn-induced ED)** — can't get hard with partner, can with porn, knows something is wrong
4. **Anxiety-loop ED** — physically capable but mind sabotages every encounter
5. **Mid-life decline** — used to work, doesn't anymore, watches sleep/stress/weight slip too
6. **Couple in a rut** — both partners disengaged, low desire, want to reconnect
7. **Optimizer** — already functional, wants to last longer / feel more / have better orgasms
8. **Recovering from a specific event** — failed encounter, traumatic experience, postpartum partner

MVP serves Personas Zero, 1, 2, 3 fully. Personas 4-8 get partial coverage and a "we hear you" message.

## 3. The twelve principles

Each principle is sourced from direct evidence in the research and audit. Together they are the product's North Star. Print them. Read them before any design decision.

**1. Health screening before selling.** Borrow Mojo's intake structure. Ask about pelvic pain, numbness, extreme thirst, weight loss, tremors, heart rate, prescriptions, mood, anxiety. If a red flag fires — no morning erections, sudden onset ED, suicidal ideation, untreated metabolic symptoms — refer to a doctor before offering a single training screen. Cite the research. We don't sell to people who need medical care.

**2. Free tier that actually works.** Pelvic floor identification, basic Kegels, basic reverse Kegels, one breathwork audio, one psychoeducation module. Free forever. Not a 7-day timer. Trust converts; trial-clocks coerce.

**3. No fear-driven copy. Ever.** Banned words and patterns: "porn destroys relationships", "you'll lose your manhood", "you're wasting your life", countdown timers, fake one-time offers, doom-date predictions, ruined-life imagery. If a copy line could appear in QUITTR's onboarding, it doesn't appear in ours.

**4. Education before assessment.** Show the user what a pelvic floor is and why it matters before asking forty questions. Use Dr. Kegel's Instagram-story format for anatomy lessons. The user should feel smarter five screens in, not more diagnosed.

**5. Coral-tier visual warmth.** Calm palette. Soft motion. No red, no urgency, no aggression. Encrypted-and-private messaging upfront. The product looks like a wellness app, not an intervention.

**6. Mojo's voice register.** "85% of erection issues in healthy young men are psychological, and that's what we're specialists in. But just in case it's physical, let's check your general health. Don't worry: if something comes up that we can't help with, we'll let you know." Confident in what we do, honest about what we don't, citation-backed, no fear. Borrow this voice directly.

**7. Indian price honesty.** ₹999/year base. Sliding scale at ₹0 / ₹499 / ₹999 / ₹1,499 with Mojo's "pick what's reasonable for you" framing. UPI native. No discount-on-cancel dark patterns. No fake urgency. The price is a price.

**8. Agency over shame in every interaction.** Streaks are optional and can be turned off. Resets are blameless. Relapses get a calm "okay, here's where we restart" not a guilt trip. The user is on his own side; the app is too.

**9. Ultra-discreet mode is non-negotiable in India.** Joint family reality means an explicit Kegel app icon on a shared phone is a privacy disaster. Borrow Kegel Trainer PFEI's pattern: an app-name disguise option, a book-mode UI disguise, biometric lock by default. Build this in v1, not v2.

**10. Mood-based daily prescription.** Coral got this right. User opens the app, picks one to three feelings (anxious, disappointed, hopeful, restless), and the app prescribes a single 5–15-minute session. No infinite menu. One thing today.

**11. Free partner premium when partnered features ship.** When we launch couple mode (Phase 2), one subscription unlocks two accounts. Mojo and Coral don't do this. We will. It's a viral mechanic and an ethical one — partners should not pay twice for a tool that benefits them jointly.

**12. Progress visualization is the conversion lever.** Your direct quote about Mojo: "I don't think I will pay — there are no analyses or 'how they show your progress from that to that'." Visible, defensible, honest progress metrics convert free users to paid. Not vanity numbers. Real ones: pelvic floor strength score over time, IELT estimate (for partnered users) or arousal-control duration (for solo users), reverse Kegel hold time, breathwork consistency.

## 4. The product, defined by what it is and what it isn't

### What it is

A daily training app for men who want to fix or improve their sexual function through evidence-based exercise, breathwork, and mindset work. It treats the body and mind together. It assumes the user can change. It teaches the muscles, the nervous system, and the cognitive patterns that govern erection and ejaculation, then drills them daily for 12-26 weeks.

### What it isn't

Not a pill. Not a doctor. Not a therapist. Not a porn filter. Not a streak-shame engine. Not a community-of-strangers forum. Not a coach-on-demand. Not a content library you browse aimlessly.

### What it does in one sentence

Every day, the app gives the user one specific 5-15 minute session calibrated to his current stage, his current mood, and what he did yesterday — and tracks his pelvic floor strength, arousal control, and consistency over weeks until the function returns or improves.

### What makes it defensible

Three things. First, **Persona Zero coverage**: nobody else builds for the man without a current partner. Second, **the integrated path**: PIED + PE + ED + PFMT + reverse Kegels in one journey, where every other app picks one or two. Third, **Indian privacy**: ultra-discreet mode, UPI billing, Hindi audio (Phase 2), joint-family-aware design.

## 5. The name

After auditing twelve options against the brand brief, two finalists remain.

### Recommended: Sahaj (सहज)

Sanskrit root meaning "in one's natural state, with ease, born together." In philosophy it points to the spontaneous, unforced way of being — the opposite of strain. The app's promise in one word: return to your natural function, without forcing it.

Why it works:
- Anti-performance-pressure baked into the name
- Culturally resonant in India without being religious
- Pronounceable globally (sah-haj, two syllables)
- The app store name "Sahaj" is searchable and not crowded
- Pairs naturally with English tagline "Sahaj — train steady"
- Discreet on a home screen — nobody learns what the app is from the name alone

Risk: low brand recognition among non-Hindi speakers initially. Mitigation: tagline does the heavy lifting in English markets.

### Alternative: Steady

Direct, calming, masculine without aggression. Maps to "steady control" which is exactly what PE training teaches. Good for global English markets. Discreet on a home screen.

Risk: too generic, harder to trademark, common word in app titles already.

### Decision

Pick Sahaj. The cultural resonance is a moat. The discretion is a feature. The philosophy embedded in the name is a daily reminder of the product's ethic.

If on testing the name doesn't resonate, fall back to Steady. But ship with Sahaj.

## 6. The MVP, screen by screen

What v1 contains. Each screen is described in enough detail that you can build it from this document.

### Onboarding (10-12 screens, ~3-4 minutes)

**Screen 1 — Welcome.** Calm color palette, soft motion. One sentence: "Sahaj helps you train your body and mind for steady, confident sexual function. Welcome." One CTA: Begin. One secondary: Already have an account.

**Screen 2 — The promise.** "This will take three minutes. There is no payment until you have used the app and decided it's worth it. Your data never leaves your phone unless you turn on cloud sync." One CTA: Continue.

**Screen 3 — Education before assessment.** Story-format, Instagram-style. Three slides: what the pelvic floor is, where it sits, what it does for erection and ejaculation. Anatomy diagram. No questions yet. The user learns first.

**Screen 4 — Persona routing.** "Which describes you right now?"
- I have a partner now and we are sexually active
- I have a partner now and we are not sexually active
- I am single and have been sexually active in the past
- I am single and have never been sexually active
- I prefer not to say

This is the Persona Zero gateway. Choices 2, 3, 4, 5 route to a solo-first flow. Choices 1, 2 unlock partnered features (Phase 2).

**Screen 5 — Goal selection.** "What brings you here?" Multi-select.
- I finish too quickly
- I struggle to get or stay hard
- I'm preparing for my first time or after a long gap
- I want to fix my relationship with porn
- I want to last longer and feel more even though things are okay
- I'm not sure yet, I'm exploring

**Screen 6 — The Mojo-style health screen.** This is the most important screen in the app. Adapt verbatim from Mojo's intake. Single question per screen, calm framing. Ask about:
- Morning erections (yes/no/sometimes)
- Pain or numbness in pelvic area
- Recent unexplained weight loss > 5kg
- Persistent thirst, frequent urination
- Chest pain or breathlessness during exertion
- Tremors, heart rate consistently high
- Current prescriptions (text input)
- Recent mood: nervous, sad, hopeless, on edge (PHQ-2 + GAD-2 short form)

**Screen 7 — Red-flag triage.** If any red flag fires, this screen appears: "Some of what you described should be checked by a doctor before training. We're not a medical service, and we want you to be well first. Here are a few options [link to telehealth services like Practo, Allo, or local clinics]. We'll be here when you're ready." User can still proceed to free tier; they cannot purchase Pro until they confirm they've seen a doctor or want to proceed anyway.

**Screen 8 — Sexual function baseline (calibrated to persona).**
- For partnered active users: standard PEDT (Premature Ejaculation Diagnostic Tool) and IIEF-5 (erectile function) — adapted, friendly language
- For Persona Zero (solo): different battery focused on solo arousal control, masturbation patterns, mental rehearsal, future-oriented anxiety

**Screen 9 — Mind/body baseline.** Stress, sleep, exercise, alcohol, porn use frequency, masturbation frequency. Five questions max.

**Screen 10 — Plan reveal.** "Based on what you shared, here is your 12-week plan." Shows: weekly structure, daily commitment (5-15 min), expected milestones at 4 / 8 / 12 weeks. Honest claim: "Roughly 65-80% of users see meaningful improvement by week 12 if they train 5+ days per week."

**Screen 11 — Privacy & discreet mode setup.** "Your data lives on this device. Cloud sync is optional and encrypted. Want to disguise the app? You can rename the icon and choose Book Mode anytime." User can enable biometric lock here.

**Screen 12 — First session ready.** "Your first session is ready. It takes 7 minutes. You can do it now or schedule it. No payment, no signup wall." CTA: Start now / Schedule for later.

### Home (after onboarding)

Three navigation tabs at the bottom: **Today**, **Library**, **Me**.

**Today tab.**
- Top: greeting and current week ("Week 3 of 12 — building strength")
- Middle: today's session card with mood check-in trigger ("How are you feeling? Get today's session" → user picks 1-3 feelings → personalized session unlocks)
- Below: streak (optional, can be turned off in settings), this week's progress chart
- Bottom: quick-access tile for breathwork (90 seconds), reverse Kegel reminder

**Library tab.**
- Browse: PFMT exercises (Kegel, Reverse Kegel, Elevator, Pulse, Inhale-Hold-Exhale), Breathwork (4-7-8, box breathing, sensate focus prep), Education (modules on anatomy, the brain-erection connection, porn and dopamine, anxiety loops, partnered communication)
- Each item has duration, difficulty, and "what it trains"

**Me tab.**
- Progress dashboard: pelvic floor strength score, arousal-control duration, weekly consistency, reverse Kegel hold time
- Settings: discreet mode, biometric lock, notifications, language (English in v1, Hindi in v2), data export, delete account
- Subscription: free tier active / Pro ₹999-1499/yr — pay what you can
- About: science behind the app, citations, contact

### Daily session structure (the heart of the product)

Every session has the same shape, only the content varies.

1. **Settle (60 seconds).** Audio prompt to find a private comfortable position, breathe.
2. **Body scan (60 seconds).** Quick attention to pelvic floor, breath, jaw, shoulders.
3. **Main practice (3-10 minutes).** PFMT, reverse Kegel, breathwork, sensate rehearsal, or psychoeducation depending on day.
4. **Integration (60-90 seconds).** Brief reflection: "How did that feel?" (one tap: easier / same / harder than yesterday). Optional journal note.
5. **Tomorrow preview.** "Tomorrow we'll do X. See you then."

Total: 5-15 minutes depending on the day.

## 7. The 12-week core protocol (content outline)

This is what you need to write and record before launch.

### Weeks 1-4: Foundation

**Goal:** Find the muscles. Learn to relax them. Build basic strength.

- Anatomy modules (3): pelvic floor, the erection cycle, the ejaculation reflex
- PFMT progression: identification → isolated contraction → 3-second hold → 5-second hold → 10-second hold
- Reverse Kegel introduction (most users have never done this; it's the wedge)
- Breathwork basics (box breathing, slow exhale)
- Mindset module: the anxiety-performance loop

### Weeks 5-8: Integration

**Goal:** Connect breath, body, and arousal awareness.

- Combined Kegel + reverse Kegel sets
- Stop-start technique for solo training
- Sensate focus solo (for Persona Zero) and partnered (when applicable)
- Mindset module: porn and dopamine, the rewire timeline
- Mindset module: spectatoring (being stuck in your head during sex)

### Weeks 9-12: Mastery and transfer

**Goal:** Apply the trained capacity to real situations.

- Advanced PFMT (functional patterns: walking, climbing stairs, simulated arousal)
- Mental rehearsal: imagined scenarios with relaxed pelvic floor
- For Persona Zero: a "first encounter readiness" module covering anxiety, condom use, communication, what to do if it doesn't go perfectly
- For partnered users: communication scripts, sensate focus with partner, reintroducing intercourse
- Maintenance plan for after week 12

### Audio/video assets needed

- 30 audio sessions (5-15 min each), recorded by you initially. Improve later.
- 12 short video animations (anatomy, technique demonstrations) — use Lottie or simple After Effects, or commission cheap on Fiverr later.
- 80 short articles (300-500 words each) for the library.
- 1 onboarding video, optional.

You can write all 80 articles yourself in 4-6 weeks of evenings, with AI assistance for first drafts. You can record 30 audio sessions in two weekends with a decent USB mic and Audacity. Total content cost: ₹0 plus a ₹3000 microphone if you don't already own one.

## 8. The pricing model

Adapted from Mojo's pay-what-you-can framing, sized for India.

### Free tier — forever

- Full pelvic floor identification flow
- 8 basic Kegel exercises
- 4 reverse Kegel exercises
- 1 breathwork session
- 3 educational articles
- Mood check-in and basic progress tracking
- Ultra-discreet mode

### Pro — annual, sliding scale

"Pro unlocks the full 12-week protocol, all audio sessions, all education modules, and detailed progress tracking. Money shouldn't get in the way of finding something that works. Pick what's reasonable for you."

- ₹0 — for users who can't afford anything (cap at 10% of pro users initially)
- ₹499 — students and low income
- ₹999 — standard
- ₹1,499 — supporter (helps subsidize the ₹0 tier)

UPI, cards, Google Play Billing. No discount-on-cancel. No countdown timers. The price is a price.

### Why this works

Mojo proved this model works. Kegel Trainer PFEI proved it works in the men's health category. It signals confidence, removes the awkward "is this worth it" friction, and the ₹1,499 supporter tier creates a virtuous-cycle revenue source. Conservatively, average revenue per paying user (ARPPU) ends up around ₹650-800, which is healthy for an Indian indie app.

Target year-1 economics: 50,000 free users → 5% paid conversion = 2,500 paying users × ₹700 ARPPU = ₹17.5 lakhs / year. Achievable in 12-18 months with patient organic growth.

## 10. The validation plan

Your stated approach: "I will truly test this app. Treat me as the worst user. If he follows diligently, he sees the result." This is the right plan. Here's how to make it rigorous.

### Self-test, weeks 13-16

You are Persona Zero by your own description (haven't been sexually active in 2-3 years). Use the app daily for four weeks. Track:
- Did the daily session take 5-15 minutes as promised?
- Did you find the muscles within week 1?
- Did your reverse Kegel hold time improve from week 1 to week 4?
- Did your pelvic floor strength score change?
- Did the content feel respectful, not pathologizing?
- Did anything feel like fear-driven sales? Note and fix.
- At week 4, do you feel different — physically, mentally, emotionally?

If you don't see results in yourself by week 4, the app isn't ready. Fix and iterate.

### Beta cohort, weeks 14-20

10-20 users from public recruitment. Mix of personas if possible (try to get 3-5 Persona Zero, 3-5 Persona 1 PE, 2-3 Persona 2 PIED, 2-3 Persona 3 anxiety-loop ED).

Weekly check-in via in-app survey:
- Are you using the app? (yes / sometimes / no)
- If no, why? (one-tap reasons)
- Notice any change yet? (yes / sometimes / no)
- One thing you'd change? (free text, optional)

At week 8 of beta, run a structured 1:1 interview with the 5-7 most engaged users. Learn what's working, what's not, what's missing.

### Public launch readiness gates

Don't launch until:
- You yourself have seen results
- At least 60% of beta cohort reports meaningful change
- Top 20 friction points are fixed
- The health screening has been reviewed by at least one medical professional (consider asking a doctor on Practo for a one-time consultation, ₹500-1500, to review the screening logic — this is the only money-spend that matters at MVP)
- Privacy and data deletion actually work
- Discreet mode actually disguises the app

## 11. Risks, decisions deferred, and what we accept

### Risks

**Clinical risk.** Without a clinician advisor, the app could miss a red flag and harm someone. Mitigation: be conservative in screening, refer out aggressively, link to telehealth, get the screening flow reviewed by one doctor before launch (one-time ₹500-1500 spend). Accept that we won't have Mojo's trust signal at launch.

**Content quality risk.** 30 audio sessions and 80 articles is a lot for a solo creator. The early content will be rougher than Coral or Mojo. Mitigation: ship anyway with clear "this is v1, we're improving" messaging, and re-record/rewrite the highest-traffic content first based on usage data.

**Persona Zero unknown.** No app has built for this user before, so there's no precedent for what they need. Mitigation: you are this user. Build for yourself, hard. Iterate based on your own use and beta feedback from Persona Zero recruits.

**Discoverability.** Indian men don't search the Play Store for this category. Marketing on Reddit, Twitter, YouTube is slow. Mitigation: accept slow growth in year 1, focus on retention not acquisition.

**Predatory competitor SEO.** QUITTR, Brainbuddy, Dr Kegel will outrank you on app store search initially. Mitigation: don't compete on SEO. Compete on word-of-mouth and Reddit/forum presence where authentic recommendations matter.

### Decisions deferred to Phase 2

- Hindi audio and full localization
- Couple mode
- Community feature
- iOS build
- Health data integrations
- AI coach (probably never — keep the app human-feeling)

### What we accept

We are not Mojo. We will not have a celebrity therapist. We will not have a marketing budget. We will not have ₹4,000/year ARPPU.

We are a solo developer building the most honest, most useful, most affordable men's sexual wellness app that has ever existed in India, with a defensible Persona Zero wedge that nobody else has noticed. That's enough.
