# Sahaj — Heritage Content Framework

> The framework, themes, candidate sources, and strict rules for the heritage pieces. **Actual excerpts are sourced via the verified public-domain pipeline** (GRETIL / sacred-texts.com / Project Madurai), with provenance confirmed per piece — that short curation pass is the remaining step, and it's ₹0.

---

## What heritage pieces are — and aren't
- Short, reflective cultural pieces from the Indian tradition, offered as moments of calm and perspective.
- **Not health advice.** They sit *outside* the medical gate (no doctor sign-off — decision #15) precisely because they carry **zero claims**. That exemption only holds if they stay claim-free.
- Not religious instruction or proselytising — respectful cultural heritage, inclusive in tone.

## Themes (aligned to Sahaj, all claim-free)
- **Ease / *sahaj*** — naturalness, effortlessness (the app's namesake).
- **Equanimity / steadiness of mind** — acting without anxiety over the outcome. (Resonates with performance anxiety, but framed as reflection, *never* as treatment.)
- **Moderation & balance.**
- **Gentle self-mastery** — discipline without punishment.
- **Stillness, breath, calm.**
- **The body as something worth caring for** — a value, not a claim.

Keep the set Indian-rooted to match the app's India-aware identity. Each excerpt **short** — a single couplet or verse.

## Strict rules — non-negotiable (this is what keeps heritage outside the medical gate)
1. **Zero claim-bearing sentences.** No "this practice improves / cures / treats…"; no implication the text is medical or sexual-health guidance. Pure cultural reflection.
2. **Era-tag every piece** (tradition + approximate date).
3. **Verified public-domain translation only.** Confirm provenance per excerpt before use; pull from the pipeline, never paste an unverified modern translation.
4. **Keep excerpts short.**
5. **Frame as "a reflection from the tradition,"** offered for perspective and calm — never as instruction or a fix.
6. **Respectful and inclusive** — not devotional instruction, not proselytising.
7. **No claim that any tradition "knew" modern medicine.**

## Sourcing pipeline (same as the Sanatan project)
- GRETIL (Sanskrit / TEI), sacred-texts.com (public-domain translations), Project Madurai (Tamil).
- Commit source files for reproducibility; tag each with its translation + date to confirm public-domain status.

## In-app
- In the content library, visually distinct from the doctor-gated articles.
- **No "review pending" badge** (they're not medical) — but only because rule 1 holds.
