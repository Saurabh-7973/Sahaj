# Sahaj — Safety & Screening Pack (the ₹0 path)

> Three pieces that let you launch **without paid professionals**, by leaning on published, authoritative sources and a conservative "when unsure, send them to a doctor" bias. Bring a clinician and lawyer in to confirm these at **first revenue** — not before launch.
>
> **None of this is diagnosis.** The app never tells a user what's wrong with them; it only, conservatively, routes them to a professional or to the safer training path. *Italic in brackets* = build/design note.

Sahaj is an **educational and training tool**, not medical care. It doesn't diagnose anything, it isn't a substitute for a doctor, and using it doesn't create a doctor–patient relationship.

- **Check with a doctor or a pelvic-floor physiotherapist before you start** — especially if you have pain, a medical condition, or have had pelvic surgery, injury, or a neurological condition.

## 2. Tension / hypertonic screening — *onboarding, between the red-flag screen and plan generation*

**Why this exists:** the program is strengthen-first, which suits most men — but for men whose pelvic floor is already **over-tight (hypertonic)** rather than weak, strengthening first can make things worse. The right starting point for them is relaxation / down-training, often with a professional. This screen catches that pattern and routes them away from the default.

### The questions *(plain, non-diagnostic)*
After the red-flag screen, for users who pass it, ask:

- Would you describe your main issue as **tension, clenching, or pain** — rather than weakness?
- Do you get **pain or pressure** in the pelvis, the perineum (between scrotum and anus), or genitals — for example when sitting?
- **Pain during or after sex or ejaculation?**
- **Urinary urgency or frequency**, trouble starting your stream, or a feeling you can't empty fully — with no diagnosed cause?
- Do you carry a lot of physical tension (jaw, shoulders) or live with chronic stress?

### Routing
- **Two or more "yes" →** show: *"From your answers, your pelvic floor may be more tight than weak. Starting with strengthening could make that worse. The better first step is gentle relaxation work — ideally with a doctor or pelvic-floor physiotherapist. We've put you on a relaxation-first version of the plan; please get this checked when you can."* → start the user on a **down-training-first track** *(reorder the program so Week-6-style lengthening leads; strengthening introduced only later/after assessment)*, OR, if that track isn't built yet for v1, **hold the plan and recommend assessment first** while keeping free articles open.
- **Pain reported anywhere →** this is already handled by the red-flag screen (§3): pain routes to a doctor first, regardless.
- **Fewer than two, no pain →** proceed to the normal strengthening plan.

*[This is the cheap fix that covers the main risk a physiotherapist review would have raised. A physio can refine the thresholds and the down-training track at first revenue.]*

## 3. Conservative red-flag triage — *onboarding (the health screen, Screen 7→9)*

**Bias:** conservative. When in doubt, route to a doctor. These are standard, published warning signs, not bespoke judgement.

### Questions *(yes/no)*
- Did your erection difficulties come on **suddenly**, rather than gradually?
- Do you have **heart disease, diabetes, or high blood pressure** (or are over 40 with these risks) **and** new erection difficulty?
- Any **blood in your urine or semen**?
- Any **lump, swelling, or pain in the testicles**?
- **Persistent pain** in the pelvis, genitals, or perineum?
- **Pain when passing urine**, or a big change in your urine stream?
- Did the symptoms start **after an injury, surgery, or a new medication**?

### Routing
- **Any "yes" on the above →** Screen 9: *"Based on your answers, it's worth seeing a doctor before starting a training programme — not because something's necessarily wrong, but because it's the right order. You can still read the general articles for free."* (Never a diagnosis.)

### Urgent / emergency carve-out — *route differently*
If the user indicates any of these, show an **urgent-care** message, not the ordinary "see a doctor soon":
- An **erection that won't go down / has lasted hours and is painful** (this can be a medical emergency).
- **Numbness in the genital or saddle area, or new weakness/numbness in the legs.**

*Message:* *"This can need urgent medical attention — please contact a doctor or emergency services now (112 in India)."*

*(The mental-health crisis interrupt — Screen 8 — is separate and complementary: it handles distress / self-harm, this handles physical red flags. Both can fire.)*
