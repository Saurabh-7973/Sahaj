# Sahaj — Pelvic-Floor Physiotherapist Review Brief

**What we're asking:** a clinical safety review of a 12-week pelvic-floor training program for men, before any users follow it. This is *not* a review of medical claims (those are in separate, separately-reviewed articles) — it is specifically about whether the **exercise progression is safely designed and sequenced**.

---

## What the app is (1 paragraph)
Sahaj is a men's pelvic-floor and sexual-wellness *training* app (self-guided, Android). Core ethic: health before performance, agency over shame, no fear-based framing. The program is rule-based: a daily ~10-minute session (breathing settle → core work → down-regulate → one reflection), 6 sessions/week with at least one rest day, over 12 weeks in three phases — Foundation (1–4), Build (5–8), Integrate (9–12). Sessions are designed to be done seated, clothed, and unnoticed. A conservative red-flag screen runs at onboarding; sessions carry repeated "small and gentle," "never strain," and "pain → stop → see a doctor" guidance.

## The questions we need answered

1. **Sequencing — the big one.** The program is strengthen-first: five weeks of contraction work before down-training arrives in Week 6. For men whose problem is a **hypertonic / over-active** pelvic floor (a meaningful share of men with ED/PE/pelvic pain), is leading with strengthening inappropriate or counterproductive? **Do we need a screening branch at onboarding that routes likely-hypertonic users to down-training first** (or to a clinician) instead of the default strengthening track? If so, what would the screening questions be?

2. **Frequency & volume.** Is **6 sessions/week** appropriate for the pelvic floor, or should it be lower? Are the **hold durations (build to 10s), rep counts, and combination patterns** sound, or over/under-dosed? Is the rest-day guidance sufficient?

3. **The down-training cue (Week 6).** It's cued as a gentle lengthening on the in-breath, with explicit, repeated "gentle only — never a forceful bearing-down / Valsalva" guardrails. **Is the cue safe and correct as written?** Any phrasing that risks straining?

4. **Contraindications / modifications.** Anyone who **should not** follow this self-guided, or who needs a modified version (e.g., post-surgical, chronic pelvic pain, neurological conditions)? Should any of these be screened out at onboarding?

5. **The onboarding red-flag triage.** We will share the draft red-flag screening questions separately. **What symptoms should conservatively route a user to a doctor before starting**, and what's the right wording so it informs without alarming?

6. **Anything missing** that a responsible self-guided pelvic-floor program for men should include and currently doesn't.
