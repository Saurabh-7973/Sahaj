# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: [date] · Applies to: Sahaj (Android)**

Sahaj ("we", "the app") is a men's pelvic-floor and sexual-wellness training app. We built it privacy-first, because this is sensitive. This policy explains, in plain language, what we do and don't do with your information.

## The short version
- Your health and training information stays **on your device**. We don't send it to a server we can read, and we don't sell it.
- We collect only limited, standard app analytics and crash data, to keep the app working.
- You can export or permanently delete your data at any time, from inside the app.

## What we collect
- **On-device data (stays on your phone):** your onboarding answers, plan, session logs, reflections, and progress. This is stored locally on your device. We do not transmit it to ourselves or read it.
- **Analytics & crash diagnostics:** we use Google Firebase Analytics and Crashlytics to understand how the app is used and to fix crashes. This may include device type, app version, and anonymous usage events. *[Confirm exact fields collected before publishing.]*
- **Purchases:** payments are processed by Google Play (and RevenueCat for subscription management). We do **not** receive or store your card details.
- **Account information:** *[only if accounts are used — describe; otherwise state "Sahaj does not require an account."]*

## What we never do
- We never sell your data.
- We never share your data with advertisers, and the app shows no ads.
- We never read your private health inputs — they don't leave your device in a form we can access.

## How your data is protected
- Local-first storage on your device.
- An optional fingerprint/face or PIN lock for the app.
- A discreet ("Book Mode") display option for privacy in shared spaces.

## Your controls and rights
- **Export** your data from within the app.
- **Delete everything** ("wipe all") from within the app, permanently.

## Children
Sahaj is for adults only (18+). It is not directed at children, and we do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18.
