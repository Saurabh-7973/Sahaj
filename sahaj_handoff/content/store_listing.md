## Full description

Sahaj (meaning "natural ease") is a quiet, private app for men who want to look after their pelvic-floor and sexual health — without pressure, jargon, or shame.

It's built on one idea: being healthy matters far more than being good at sex. So Sahaj is health-first, never performance-first, and there's no fear-mongering anywhere in it.

**What you get**
- A guided 12-week pelvic-floor training program — a few quiet minutes a day.
- Sessions you can do sitting down, fully clothed, with nobody around you noticing.
- A breathing-led approach that trains both strength and the ability to relax — not just one.
- Plain-language health articles, reviewed by a doctor.
- An honest progress view, where every number is explained, not inflated.

**Built for privacy**
- Your information stays on your device.
- Lock the app with your fingerprint or a PIN.
- A discreet mode makes Sahaj look like a plain reading app.

**Fair by design**
- The first four weeks are free, for as long as you want them — no trial countdown.
- After that, pay what you can. If money's tight, there's a genuine free option.
- Health questions come first; we never put money before your wellbeing.

Whether you're working on something specific or just building a healthy foundation — and whether or not you have a partner — Sahaj meets you where you are.

*Sahaj supports your health and is not a substitute for medical care. If something needs a doctor, we'll tell you.*

## What's new *(v1.0)*
First release. The full 12-week program, private by default, with a free foundation and pay-what-you-can after.

## Content rating questionnaire — prep notes *(IARC / Play)*
- Declare **references to sexual health / sexual themes in a medical-educational context**; **no** explicit sexual content, **no** nudity, **no** graphic anatomy in store assets or UI.
- Expect a **Mature 17+ / PEGI 18** style rating; that's fine and expected for this category.
- Answer honestly and conservatively; do not understate the sexual-health framing.

## Data safety form — prep notes
- **Data collected:** account/auth only if used; otherwise minimal. Health-related inputs are stored **on-device** (state this clearly).
- **Analytics/diagnostics:** Firebase Analytics + Crashlytics only (paid trackers are off). Disclose as collected for analytics/crash reporting; describe purpose plainly.
- **Sharing:** none sold; none shared with third parties for ads.
- **Security:** on-device storage; optional biometric/PIN lock.
- **Deletion:** in-app data export and wipe-all available — surface this.
- Keep the form's claims exactly in sync with what the app actually does before submission.

## Listing guardrails

- No medical claims in the description (the doctor-gated articles carry the evidence, not the store page).
