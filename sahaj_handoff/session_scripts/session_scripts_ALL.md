# Sahaj — Session Scripts
## Week 1: Foundation (Awareness)

> Convention for this file:
> **Plain text** = copy shown/spoken to the user.
> *Italic in brackets* = implementation note for Claude Code (timers, pacing, behaviour). Not shown to the user.

---

### Week 1 goal
Learn to *find* the pelvic floor, lift it correctly without dragging in the wrong muscles, and — most of all — fully release it. No strengthening this week. This is about sensation and control, not effort.

---

### Before you start — *show once, Week 1 Session 1 only*

A few honest ground rules:

- **Small and gentle wins.** A harder squeeze is not a better one. A pelvic floor that's always clenched causes more trouble than a weak one — so we train the *release* as carefully as the lift.
- **It should never hurt.** If anything here causes pain, stop. Pain isn't progress in this work; it's a sign to ease off, or to check with a doctor.
- **The "stop your pee" idea is only for finding the muscles.** Try it once to locate them, then don't make a habit of stopping your flow mid-stream — it's a locator, not an exercise.
- **Every session can be done sitting still, fully dressed, breathing normally.** No one around you would notice. That's by design.

---

### Session structure — *every session, all 12 weeks*
1. **Settle** (~2 min) — breathing, let tension go.
2. **Core work** (~6 min) — the week's technique.
3. **Down-regulate** (~2 min) — release fully, end calm.
4. **Reflect** — one prompt.

---

## Session 1 — full script

### Block 1 · Settle · 2:00
*[timer 2:00. Cadence cue underneath: inhale ~4s, exhale ~6s — longer out than in. Lines reveal slowly, unhurried.]*

Sit however you're comfortable — a chair, the edge of a bed. Let your shoulders drop.

Rest one hand on your belly.

Breathe in slowly through your nose, and let the belly rise into your hand — not the chest.

Now let it out, slow and long, like a quiet sigh.

Again. In, the belly rises... out, longer than the in.

Keep going at your own pace. With each out-breath, let a little more go — your jaw, your shoulders, the area between your sit bones.

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — Finding the muscles
*[timer 6:00. Session 1 is spacious — mostly breathing, only a handful of gentle cycles with long rests. No rep counter shown.]*

We're going to find the pelvic floor. Two ways to locate it:

Imagine you badly need to pass gas and have to hold it in — that small internal lift.

Or the muscles you'd use to stop yourself peeing midstream. (Only to find them — don't actually do that while peeing as a habit.)

That gentle draw up and in — that's the muscle.

Now the important part: keep it *small*. We're learning the feeling, not lifting anything heavy.

And keep breathing. If you catch yourself holding your breath, you're trying too hard.

Quick check — rest your other hand on your belly or your buttock. They should stay soft. Only the floor inside works. Not the stomach, not the glutes, not the thighs.

Let's try a few, slow and easy.

On your next out-breath — lift, just a little.

And on the in-breath — let it go. All the way. Completely soft.

Feel that difference? Lifted... and fully let go. The letting-go is half the skill — maybe the more important half.

Again, no rush. Out-breath, gentle lift... in-breath, full release.

*[continue ~5–6 unhurried cycles, generous rest between each]*

If you can barely feel anything yet, that's completely normal. It's week one. The feeling gets clearer quickly.

### Block 3 · Down-regulate · 2:00
*[timer 2:00.]*

Stop lifting now. Let everything down there go heavy and soft — more relaxed than when you started.

A few more slow breaths. Long out-breaths.

Nothing to do here, nothing to achieve. Just sitting, breathing, calm.

That's the session.

### Reflect — one prompt
**Could you tell the difference between holding and fully letting go?**
- Yes, clearly
- A little
- Not yet — and that's fine

---

## Sessions 2–6 — deltas from Session 1

Same three-block shape; **Settle** and **Down-regulate** are unchanged. Only **Core work** moves:

- **Session 2** — Repeat Session 1's finding work exactly. Locating takes a few goes; today it just needs to feel more familiar than yesterday. Reflect prompt unchanged.

- **Session 3** — First gentle *holds*. Lift on the out-breath, hold softly for **2 seconds** while still breathing, then release on the in-breath and rest **at least 4 seconds** — rest longer than you held. ~6 reps. Cue line: "the rest matters more than the hold."

- **Session 4** — As Session 3, ~8 reps. Add the check: if the belly or glutes tense to help, that's too much — make the lift smaller, not bigger.

- **Session 5** — Holds of **3 seconds**, rest 5–6 seconds, ~8 reps. Reflect prompt changes to: **"Was the release as easy as the lift?"** *(The answer should be yes. If the release feels harder than the lift, that's the thing worth noticing.)*

- **Session 6** — As Session 5, ~10 reps. Closing line for the week: acknowledge — quietly, no fanfare — that they've just spent a week learning the foundation most men never learn, on their own terms.

---

## Rest day — *1× this week, user picks the day*
Not "skipping." Muscle learns on the rest day too. A chosen rest day is **on-plan** — no "missed day" flag, no gap-adjustment copy. *(Ties to decision #2.)*

## Week 2 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — Two gears

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1.]*

Last week was about finding the muscle and letting it go. This week we start working it — in two gears.

**First gear — slow holds.**

Same as the end of last week, a touch longer. On the out-breath, lift — small and clean. Hold it, gently, for **4 seconds**, and keep breathing through the hold. Then release on the in-breath, all the way, and rest about **6 seconds**.

Form rules haven't changed: small lift, breathing easy, belly and glutes and thighs soft. Only the floor works.

Let's do about **6**, no rush, full release every time.

*[pace ~6 reps: 4s hold / ~6s rest, breath-coordinated]*

Good. Shake the legs out if you like. Now a completely different gear.

**Second gear — quick flicks.** *(new)*

These are fast. Squeeze and let go right away — like flicking a light switch on, then off.

One thing changes here: don't try to time these to your breath. They're too quick for that. Just keep breathing normally the whole time — easy, not held.

And even fast, the let-go has to be complete. Squeeze, full release. Squeeze, full release.

Let's do about **8**.

*[pace ~8 quick flicks: ~1s on / ~1s off; breathing free, NOT synced]*

If that got muddy after a few, or the muscle stopped answering — stop there. That's completely normal. The fast fibres tire quickly at first; stamina comes.

*[if time/energy remains, optional second short round of each — otherwise close]*

# Sahaj — Session Scripts
## Week 3: Control (The Elevator)

> Convention: **plain text** = copy shown/spoken to the user. *Italic in brackets* = implementation note for Claude Code, not shown to the user.

---

### Week 3 goal
Add **control** to the slow gear. Instead of on/off, you learn to lift *and lower* in stages — and to do the work in more than one position. The slow, controlled way down is the real lesson this week.

---

### New this week — *short callout, Week 3 Session 1 only*
- You have two gears. Now we add control to the slow one.
- The lift becomes graded — up in steps, and back down in steps.
- Coming down slowly, in control, matters more than the lift. It's the opposite of gripping.

---

### Session structure — *unchanged*
Settle (~2 min) → Core work (~6 min) → Down-regulate (~2 min) → one reflection prompt.
Settle and Down-regulate reuse the Week 1 copy. Only Core work and the reflection change below.

---

## Week 3 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — The elevator + quick flicks

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1.]*

You've got two gears now — slow holds and quick flicks. This week we add control to the slow one. Instead of just on and off, you'll lift and lower in stages.

**The elevator.** *(new)*

Think of the lift like an elevator with a few floors.

On the out-breath, lift gently to floor one — just a little. Pause.

A bit more — floor two. Pause.

And up to the top — floor three, your comfortable max. Still small overall, still breathing. Hold a couple of seconds.

Now the part that matters most. Come back **down** in the same steps. Don't drop. Floor two... pause... floor one... pause... all the way to the bottom, soft.

Lowering slowly and in control is its own skill — the opposite of gripping. Give the way down as much care as the way up.

Let's do about **4 or 5**, resting between.

*[pace ~4–5 graded reps: stepped lift, ~2s top hold, stepped controlled descent]*

If you only really feel one or two floors, that's completely fine. "A little, then a bit more, then slowly down" *is* the exercise. And if you're holding your breath to manage the floors, make the whole thing smaller.

Short reset. Now the fast gear, unchanged.

**Quick flicks.** Crisp on, full off. Breathing free, not synced. Two rounds of about **10**.

*[2×10 quick flicks, brief rest between rounds]*

**One more thing — position.** Do one round of the elevator sitting, then stand up and do one standing. Same muscle; it feels a little different standing, and that's normal.

That's the session.

### Reflect — one prompt
**On the way down, could you lower in steps — or did it drop all at once?**
- In clear steps
- Somewhat
- It just dropped — *(then: that's exactly the thing to practise)*

## Sessions 2–6 — deltas from Week 3 · Session 1

Settle and Down-regulate unchanged. Core work:

- **Session 2** — Repeat Session 1. Let the up-and-down grading start to feel smoother. Reflect unchanged.

- **Session 3** — Elevator with a slightly longer top hold (**~3s**), ~5 reps. New cue: "try to make the floors even — the same gap between each, up and down." Quick flicks 2×10.

- **Session 4** — As Session 3, then add a **moving** set: after the seated elevators, do ~5 quick flicks while slowly walking a few steps. Cue the discretion: "walking and flicking — the kind of thing you do crossing a room, with no one able to tell."

- **Session 5** — Elevator **sitting and standing** as standard now (one round each), ~3s top hold, ~5 reps per position. Quick flicks 2×10, one round standing. Reflect shifts to: **"Standing vs sitting — could you find the muscle as easily standing?"** (Yes / Harder standing / Couldn't really standing — *standing is genuinely harder, that's normal*).

- **Session 6** — Full mix: elevator sitting + standing, quick flicks including a few during a slow walk. Closing line: three weeks in — they now have control (grading it), speed (flicks), and they're starting to use it on the move, quietly, in real life. A plain acknowledgement that the foundation is genuinely built.

### Week 4 goal
Tie the foundation together into a routine you own, and add the one thing that makes it useful: **timing**. You'll learn "the knack" — bringing a contraction in *before* a moment, not after.

---

### New this week — *short callout, Week 4 Session 1 only*
- Last week of the foundation. You've learned a lot.
- This week ties it together — and adds timing.
- "The knack" is a well-timed contraction *ahead* of a moment. It's the difference between an exercise and a muscle that's there when you want it.

---

### Session structure — *unchanged*

## Week 4 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — Your routine + the knack

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1. Coaching is deliberately lighter this week — more autonomy, fewer step-by-step cues.]*

This is the last week of the foundation. You can find the muscle, work it in two gears, and control it up and down. This week we tie it together — and add timing.

**First, your routine.** You know these now, so I'll step back. Run through them at your own pace:

A few elevators — up in steps, slowly down — sitting.

A round of quick flicks.

Then the same, standing.

Find your own rhythm. If you've landed on a version that feels right, trust it.

*[allow ~2.5 min self-paced; minimal cueing]*

**Now, the knack.** *(new)*

So far you've done reps for their own sake. The knack is different — it's timing a contraction to a moment, *before* it happens, not after.

The textbook example is a gentle lift just before you cough or sneeze — bracing before the pressure arrives.

Let's practise with something simple: standing up. Sit forward on your seat. Just before you rise — lift the floor, gently. Hold it as you stand. Then let go once you're up.

Anticipate... contract... hold through the moment... release after.

Try it a couple more times — sit, prepare, stand with the lift already there.

*[~3 timed sit-to-stand reps with pre-contraction]*

Bringing it in *ahead* of the moment is the whole point. That timing is what turns this from an exercise into control you can actually use.

- I kept catching it late — *(then: that's the skill itself; it comes with practice)*

## Week 6: Down-Training (The Other Direction)

> Convention: **plain text** = copy shown/spoken to the user. *Italic in brackets* = implementation note for Claude Code, not shown to the user.
>
> *Most safety-sensitive week so far. The lengthening cue must stay gentle — never a forceful bearing-down / Valsalva. Guardrails below are not optional copy.*

---

### Week 6 goal
Learn the other direction. Five weeks of lifting; this week, actively **lengthening and releasing** the floor — letting it soften and open on demand, not just relax passively. Strong is only half of it. This week makes the muscle supple too.

---

### New this week — *short callout, Week 6 Session 1 only*
- We've spent five weeks learning to lift. This week, the opposite: letting the floor gently lengthen and open.
- A muscle that's always braced causes its own trouble. Being able to fully let go is real control — for many men, the more important half.
- We ease off the lifting this week to focus here. That's deliberate, not a step back.

### Important — *show with the down-training block, Week 6, every session*
**Gentle only. This is a soft letting-go, never a hard push.** If you're straining, bearing down with force, or red in the face, you've gone too far — ease right off. It should feel like *more* relaxation, not effort. Keep breathing. If anything hurts or feels like pressure, stop.

---

### Session structure — *unchanged*
Settle (~2 min) → Core work (~6 min) → Down-regulate (~2 min) → one reflection prompt.
Settle and Down-regulate reuse the Week 1 copy. Only Core work and the reflection change below.

## Week 6 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — Lengthening + contrast

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1. This block is unhurried and relaxed — closer in feel to the Settle block than to a workout.]*

A short bit of the lifting first, to keep what you've built — then we spend most of today on the other direction.

**Keep the strength ticking over.** A few easy endurance holds — about 5 seconds, lift small, breathe, lower in control. Just **5 or 6**. We're maintaining today, not pushing.

*[~5–6 reps, 5s holds, controlled descent — light volume by design]*

Now, the new thing.

**Lengthening — the other direction.** *(new)*

*[show the "Gentle only" guardrail callout here]*

So far, every contraction has gone up. Now we let the floor go *down* — gently lengthen and open it, more than just relaxing.

It works with the in-breath. As you breathe in and your belly expands, let the floor gently lengthen and soften downward, as if it's quietly opening. On the out-breath, return to neutral — not a lift, just easy and soft.

The feeling is subtle — a gentle downward release, like the very start of letting go more fully. **Gentle.** No pushing, no force.

Let's do a few, slow. In — let it lengthen and open... out — back to soft neutral.

*[~5–6 gentle reps, breath-led, fully relaxed, long pauses]*

If you can barely tell anything's happening, that's completely fine — this one is subtle and takes practice to feel.

**Now the contrast.** Feeling the whole range:

Gently lift — a small kegel. Back to neutral. Now gently lengthen and open — down. Back to neutral.

Up... neutral... down... neutral. Smooth, no force in either direction.

*[~4–5 contrast reps]*

Strength without the ability to fully let go isn't the goal. Now your muscle can do both.

- After the final session, the app moves to **maintenance mode**, not an ending. Home becomes the user's saved maintenance plan (Week 10); the library and articles stay accessible; on-demand sessions and the gentle gap-restart path remain available. Never a dead-end, never a nag.
- Graduation must trigger **no upsell, renewal, or retention prompt** of any kind. If anything paid exists beyond the program, it is not surfaced here. Funnelling the finish line into a sale is exactly the pattern this app refuses.
- The "note to future self" is saved and, if the user returns after a long absence, surfaced gently as part of a warm, no-guilt restart — tying back to the Week 11 resilience work.
