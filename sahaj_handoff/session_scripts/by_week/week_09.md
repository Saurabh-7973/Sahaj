## Week 9 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — On demand + presence

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1.]*

The building's done. From here it's about using this in real life. Two things today.

**On demand — no warm-up.**

Real moments don't wait for you to get ready. So: right now, cold — engage the floor. Gently, cleanly. ... And release, fully.

Again, no ramp-up. Engage... release. Quick to answer, clean to let go.

*[~6 cold engage/release reps, randomised short intervals, no build-up]*

A few quick flicks and one full-length hold, just to keep things ticking — then we move to the part that matters most.

*[brief maintenance: ~6 flicks + 1–2 long holds]*

**Presence — control without bracing.** *(the key one)*

Here's the skill that counts when it's real. Engage the floor — gently — and at the same time, check the rest of you: jaw loose, shoulders down, breath easy. Control in one place, relaxed everywhere else.

That combination — calm body, gentle control — is worth more than any strong squeeze. Hold it a moment... and let go.

*[~4 reps: gentle engage + active full-body relaxation + release]*

**Out in your life this week:** use it in the real moments — a lift, a stressful spike, whenever you want it there. And whatever "the situations that matter" means for you. If that doesn't involve a partner right now, that's completely fine — the everyday steadiness and the calm are the whole point. If it does, the most useful thing is the same: stay relaxed and present, not clenched and not performing. Closeness isn't a test. *(For how this connects to sexual health specifically, the articles cover it carefully and honestly.)*
