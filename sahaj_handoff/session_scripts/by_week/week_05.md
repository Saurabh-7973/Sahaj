## Week 5 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — Longer holds + hold-and-pulse

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1.]*

Welcome to the build phase. You've got a real foundation — now we load it a bit more and start combining the gears.

**Endurance holds, a little longer.**

Lift to a comfortable hold — small as ever — and this time hold for about **6 seconds**, breathing the whole way. Then lower in control, the way you learned: down in stages, never a drop.

Let's do about **8**, resting between. If the lift creeps bigger to keep the hold going, that's recruiting — keep it small and let it be a shorter hold instead.

*[~8 reps: 6s hold / controlled descent / rest ~6s]*

Good. Shake out, reset. Now a new pattern.

**Hold and pulse.** *(new)*

Lift to a comfortable base hold — about half your max. Keep that base steady.

Now, on top of it, add a few quick pulses — pulse up and back to the base, without losing the base. Pulse... pulse... pulse... the base never drops.

Then lower the whole thing down, slowly.

That's one. The base hold staying put while you pulse — that's the skill. Let's do about **4**, with good rests; this one's taxing.

*[~4 reps: base hold + 3–4 pulses on top + controlled descent; generous rest]*

One round of plain **quick flicks** to finish — crisp on, full off, breathing free. About **10**.

*[1×10 quick flicks]*

And keep the knack going out in your day — before a cough, standing up. It doesn't only live in here.

### Reflect — one prompt
**During hold-and-pulse — did the base hold stay put while you pulsed, or did it sag?**
- Stayed steady
- Sagged a little
- Lost it — *(then: that's the hard part of this one; it comes with reps)*
