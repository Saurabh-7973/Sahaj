# Sahaj — Session Scripts
## Week 6: Down-Training (The Other Direction)

> Convention: **plain text** = copy shown/spoken to the user. *Italic in brackets* = implementation note for Claude Code, not shown to the user.
>
> *Most safety-sensitive week so far. The lengthening cue must stay gentle — never a forceful bearing-down / Valsalva. Guardrails below are not optional copy.*

---

### Week 6 goal
Learn the other direction. Five weeks of lifting; this week, actively **lengthening and releasing** the floor — letting it soften and open on demand, not just relax passively. Strong is only half of it. This week makes the muscle supple too.

---

### New this week — *short callout, Week 6 Session 1 only*
- We've spent five weeks learning to lift. This week, the opposite: letting the floor gently lengthen and open.
- A muscle that's always braced causes its own trouble. Being able to fully let go is real control — for many men, the more important half.
- We ease off the lifting this week to focus here. That's deliberate, not a step back.

### Important — *show with the down-training block, Week 6, every session*
**Gentle only. This is a soft letting-go, never a hard push.** If you're straining, bearing down with force, or red in the face, you've gone too far — ease right off. It should feel like *more* relaxation, not effort. Keep breathing. If anything hurts or feels like pressure, stop.

---

### Session structure — *unchanged*
Settle (~2 min) → Core work (~6 min) → Down-regulate (~2 min) → one reflection prompt.
Settle and Down-regulate reuse the Week 1 copy. Only Core work and the reflection change below.

## Week 6 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — Lengthening + contrast

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1. This block is unhurried and relaxed — closer in feel to the Settle block than to a workout.]*

A short bit of the lifting first, to keep what you've built — then we spend most of today on the other direction.

**Keep the strength ticking over.** A few easy endurance holds — about 5 seconds, lift small, breathe, lower in control. Just **5 or 6**. We're maintaining today, not pushing.

*[~5–6 reps, 5s holds, controlled descent — light volume by design]*

Now, the new thing.

**Lengthening — the other direction.** *(new)*

*[show the "Gentle only" guardrail callout here]*

So far, every contraction has gone up. Now we let the floor go *down* — gently lengthen and open it, more than just relaxing.

It works with the in-breath. As you breathe in and your belly expands, let the floor gently lengthen and soften downward, as if it's quietly opening. On the out-breath, return to neutral — not a lift, just easy and soft.

The feeling is subtle — a gentle downward release, like the very start of letting go more fully. **Gentle.** No pushing, no force.

Let's do a few, slow. In — let it lengthen and open... out — back to soft neutral.

*[~5–6 gentle reps, breath-led, fully relaxed, long pauses]*

If you can barely tell anything's happening, that's completely fine — this one is subtle and takes practice to feel.

**Now the contrast.** Feeling the whole range:

Gently lift — a small kegel. Back to neutral. Now gently lengthen and open — down. Back to neutral.

Up... neutral... down... neutral. Smooth, no force in either direction.

*[~4–5 contrast reps]*

Strength without the ability to fully let go isn't the goal. Now your muscle can do both.

### Reflect — one prompt
**Could you feel the floor actively lengthen downward — not just go slack, but gently open?**
- Yes
- Maybe
- Couldn't tell the difference — *(then: that's normal; it's a subtle one, and the contrast drill makes it clearer)*

## Sessions 2–6 — deltas from Week 6 · Session 1

Settle and Down-regulate unchanged. Light up-work maintained each session (~5–6 short holds). Core work focus:

- **Session 2** — Repeat Session 1. The lengthening is subtle; today is about feeling it more clearly than yesterday. Reflect unchanged.

- **Session 3** — Add a gentle **lengthen-and-rest**: lengthen the floor and stay there for a couple of slow breaths — resting at the open, soft end of the range. Keep the guardrails front and centre.

- **Session 4** — Take it into your day: "next time you notice you're clenched — jaw, shoulders, or down there — try a conscious lengthen-and-release, right then." Down-training where it actually matters. Contrast drill in-session.

- **Session 5** — Balanced session: short up-work, focused lengthening, smooth contrast. Reflect shifts to: **"When you're tense in daily life, can you now let the floor lengthen on purpose — or is it stuck?"** (Yes / Sometimes / Stuck — *then: that's exactly what this trains, keep at it*).

- **Session 6** — Full balanced week, mostly self-directed: lift, lengthen, contrast. Closing line: you can raise it *and* lengthen it on demand now — strong and supple. The over-tension trap that catches so many men, sidestepped. A plain note that this is the week most programs skip entirely.
