### Week 11 goal
Get ready for the parts no one likes — the plateaus, the weeks you fall off, the days the muscle won't answer. And learn the most honest thing this app can tell you: when something needs a doctor, not more practice.

---

### New this week — *short callout, Week 11 Session 1 only*
- Progress isn't a straight line. Plateaus are normal, not failure.
- Falling off is normal too — and recoverable. The muscle remembers; you come back faster than you built.
- And the honest part: this helps, but it doesn't fix everything. Knowing when to see a doctor is part of looking after yourself.

## Week 11 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — For the hard parts

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1. This block is mostly honest coaching with a short maintenance run — not heavy drilling.]*

Keep it ticking first — your light maintenance run: a few holds, a round of flicks, a couple of gentle lengthens.

*[brief maintenance: ~3 holds + ~10 flicks + ~3 lengthens]*

Now the part that actually matters this week. Three honest things.

**Plateaus are normal.** After the early gains, progress slows. That isn't you failing — it's what every kind of training does. Don't grind harder to force it; trust the maintenance. Sometimes a plateau just means you've reached a good place to hold.

**Falling off is recoverable.** Life happens — you'll miss days, maybe weeks. That's not the end of anything, and it's nothing to feel bad about. When you come back, don't resume at full intensity. Drop back a couple of weeks' worth and rebuild gently — the muscle remembers, and it returns faster than it took to build. Restarting *is* the practice. There's no version of this where you've "ruined it."

**And the honest one.** This helps a lot of things, but it doesn't fix everything, and it would be dishonest to pretend otherwise. If you've practised consistently and something still isn't improving — or there's pain, or something just feels off — that's a doctor, not more training. Seeing a doctor is the strong move, not the failure.

*(The warning-signs article walks through this carefully and calmly — what's worth a check, and what isn't. Worth a read.)*

## Sessions 2–6 — deltas from Week 11 · Session 1

Settle and Down-regulate unchanged. Core work focus:

- **Session 2** — Repeat Session 1. Let the restart approach and the plateau reframe sink in. Reflect unchanged.

- **Session 3** — **The restart, practised.** Run an actual gentle rebuild session, as if returning from a gap: shorter holds, fewer reps, easy. So the re-entry is something you've done, not just heard about. Cue: "this is exactly what coming back looks like — unremarkable, and it works."

- **Session 4** — **Bad days.** What a minimal day looks like: just the carryover, or one short set, or honest rest. A bad day is not a verdict on you or your progress. No harsh self-talk — "I've blown it" is never true here. *(Ties to mood calibration: a low day flexes the session, it doesn't cancel you.)*

- **Session 5** — **The doctor line, clearly.** The most important session this week. Repeat the honest message: consistent practice and no improvement, or pain, or something off → a doctor, and that's wisdom, not weakness. Point to the warning-signs article for the specifics; don't list symptoms here. Reflect shifts to: **"Do you know the difference between 'keep practising' and 'this needs a doctor'?"** (Yes / Roughly / Not really — *then: the warning-signs article lays it out, calmly; knowing this is part of taking care of yourself*).

- **Session 6** — Carry the whole resilience mindset, self-directed. Closing line, plain: you're set up for the hard parts now — plateaus, gaps, restarts — and you know when something's beyond training. That's not weakness; it's looking after yourself properly. *(One week left.)*

## Impl note — the doctor message *(not user-facing)*
- The "see a doctor" guidance is **provider-agnostic** — their GP, any clinician, whoever they choose. It must **not** be wired to surface, suggest, or link the paid in-app consultation. Turning an honest health message into a sales path is exactly the fear-to-paywall pattern the app's ethic forbids.
- Specifics (what's a warning sign vs not) live only in the doctor-gated warning-signs article, behind its "review pending" badge until sign-off. Session copy stays general and calm.
