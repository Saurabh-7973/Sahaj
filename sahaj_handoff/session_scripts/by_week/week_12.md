## Session 6 — Graduation close *(copy, shown after the final session)*

That's twelve weeks. You're done.

Twelve weeks ago, this was a muscle you couldn't reliably find. Now it's strong, it's supple, it answers when you call it, and it lives in days you were already living. That's real — and you built it. The app only held the map.

Here's the honest part, and it's the whole reason this exists: this was never about being good at sex. It was about your health, and about being in charge of your own body. Being well matters more than being impressive. If you keep one thing from here, keep that.

And here's the part most apps would never say out loud: you don't need this anymore. You have your routine, your anchors, your way back if you ever fall off, and the judgement to know when something belongs to a doctor instead. Holding on to all of that without us is exactly what success looks like. We'd rather have helped you not need this than kept you opening it every day.

The door stays open — your plan is right here, the articles aren't going anywhere, and if a hard stretch knocks you off, come back and restart gently. No clock. No guilt. Use it if it helps; don't if it doesn't.

Go well.

## Impl notes — graduation handoff *(not user-facing)*
- After the final session, the app moves to **maintenance mode**, not an ending. Home becomes the user's saved maintenance plan (Week 10); the library and articles stay accessible; on-demand sessions and the gentle gap-restart path remain available. Never a dead-end, never a nag.
- Graduation must trigger **no upsell, renewal, or retention prompt** of any kind. If anything paid exists beyond the program, it is not surfaced here. Funnelling the finish line into a sale is exactly the pattern this app refuses.
- The "note to future self" is saved and, if the user returns after a long absence, surfaced gently as part of a warm, no-guilt restart — tying back to the Week 11 resilience work.
