## Week 4 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — Your routine + the knack

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1. Coaching is deliberately lighter this week — more autonomy, fewer step-by-step cues.]*

This is the last week of the foundation. You can find the muscle, work it in two gears, and control it up and down. This week we tie it together — and add timing.

**First, your routine.** You know these now, so I'll step back. Run through them at your own pace:

A few elevators — up in steps, slowly down — sitting.

A round of quick flicks.

Then the same, standing.

Find your own rhythm. If you've landed on a version that feels right, trust it.

*[allow ~2.5 min self-paced; minimal cueing]*

**Now, the knack.** *(new)*

So far you've done reps for their own sake. The knack is different — it's timing a contraction to a moment, *before* it happens, not after.

The textbook example is a gentle lift just before you cough or sneeze — bracing before the pressure arrives.

Let's practise with something simple: standing up. Sit forward on your seat. Just before you rise — lift the floor, gently. Hold it as you stand. Then let go once you're up.

Anticipate... contract... hold through the moment... release after.

Try it a couple more times — sit, prepare, stand with the lift already there.

*[~3 timed sit-to-stand reps with pre-contraction]*

Bringing it in *ahead* of the moment is the whole point. That timing is what turns this from an exercise into control you can actually use.

### Reflect — one prompt
**The knack — could you bring the contraction in *before* you stood, not after?**
- Yes
- Close
- I kept catching it late — *(then: that's the skill itself; it comes with practice)*
