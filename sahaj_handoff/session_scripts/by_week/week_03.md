### Week 3 goal
Add **control** to the slow gear. Instead of on/off, you learn to lift *and lower* in stages — and to do the work in more than one position. The slow, controlled way down is the real lesson this week.

## Week 3 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — The elevator + quick flicks

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1.]*

You've got two gears now — slow holds and quick flicks. This week we add control to the slow one. Instead of just on and off, you'll lift and lower in stages.

**The elevator.** *(new)*

Think of the lift like an elevator with a few floors.

On the out-breath, lift gently to floor one — just a little. Pause.

A bit more — floor two. Pause.

And up to the top — floor three, your comfortable max. Still small overall, still breathing. Hold a couple of seconds.

Now the part that matters most. Come back **down** in the same steps. Don't drop. Floor two... pause... floor one... pause... all the way to the bottom, soft.

Lowering slowly and in control is its own skill — the opposite of gripping. Give the way down as much care as the way up.

Let's do about **4 or 5**, resting between.

*[pace ~4–5 graded reps: stepped lift, ~2s top hold, stepped controlled descent]*

If you only really feel one or two floors, that's completely fine. "A little, then a bit more, then slowly down" *is* the exercise. And if you're holding your breath to manage the floors, make the whole thing smaller.

Short reset. Now the fast gear, unchanged.

**Quick flicks.** Crisp on, full off. Breathing free, not synced. Two rounds of about **10**.

*[2×10 quick flicks, brief rest between rounds]*

**One more thing — position.** Do one round of the elevator sitting, then stand up and do one standing. Same muscle; it feels a little different standing, and that's normal.

That's the session.

### Reflect — one prompt
**On the way down, could you lower in steps — or did it drop all at once?**
- In clear steps
- Somewhat
- It just dropped — *(then: that's exactly the thing to practise)*

## Sessions 2–6 — deltas from Week 3 · Session 1

Settle and Down-regulate unchanged. Core work:

- **Session 2** — Repeat Session 1. Let the up-and-down grading start to feel smoother. Reflect unchanged.

- **Session 3** — Elevator with a slightly longer top hold (**~3s**), ~5 reps. New cue: "try to make the floors even — the same gap between each, up and down." Quick flicks 2×10.

- **Session 4** — As Session 3, then add a **moving** set: after the seated elevators, do ~5 quick flicks while slowly walking a few steps. Cue the discretion: "walking and flicking — the kind of thing you do crossing a room, with no one able to tell."

- **Session 5** — Elevator **sitting and standing** as standard now (one round each), ~3s top hold, ~5 reps per position. Quick flicks 2×10, one round standing. Reflect shifts to: **"Standing vs sitting — could you find the muscle as easily standing?"** (Yes / Harder standing / Couldn't really standing — *standing is genuinely harder, that's normal*).

- **Session 6** — Full mix: elevator sitting + standing, quick flicks including a few during a slow walk. Closing line: three weeks in — they now have control (grading it), speed (flicks), and they're starting to use it on the move, quietly, in real life. A plain acknowledgement that the foundation is genuinely built.
