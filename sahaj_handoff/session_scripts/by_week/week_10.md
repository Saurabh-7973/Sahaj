## Week 10 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — Anchors + your plan

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1.]*

The building is behind you. The job now is keeping it — and that takes much less than you might think.

**First, the honest maintenance dose.** A short run of the essentials is plenty: a few holds, a round of flicks, a couple of gentle lengthens. Let's do exactly that — your light maintenance session, start to finish.

*[~3 holds + 1×10 flicks + ~3 lengthens — deliberately brief; this models the maintenance dose]*

That's it. That's enough to hold what you've got. You don't need the full build phase forever.

**Now the real skill: anchoring.** *(new)*

The reason habits fade is they rely on remembering and motivation. The fix is to tie them to something you already do, every single day — so the cue does the remembering for you.

A few that work well:
- Quick flicks while you brush your teeth.
- A couple of holds while the kettle boils, or waiting for the lift.
- The knack, automatically, every time you stand up.
- A gentle engage each time you walk through a doorway.

Pick one right now and try it in your head: "every time I ____, I'll ____." That sentence is the whole technique.

**Build your plan.** Over this week, settle on **two or three anchors** and the **handful of elements** you'll keep. That's your maintenance plan — light enough to actually do, woven into a day you're already living.

*[Impl: let the user select and save a personal maintenance plan — chosen elements + chosen daily anchors + a frequency. Surface it as their own going-forward routine, editable. This is a real artifact to persist, not just copy.]*

### Reflect — one prompt
**Did you tie any of this to something you already do every day — teeth, the commute, standing up?**
- Yes, found an anchor
- Tried, didn't stick yet
- Not yet — *(then: that's the work this week; one anchor is enough to start)*
