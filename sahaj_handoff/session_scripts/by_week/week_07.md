## Week 7 · Session 1 — Core work (full)

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — Peak holds + a supple check; movement goes into your day

*[timer 6:00. Settle/Down-regulate as Week 1. The movement carryover happens out in the user's day — see cues — not crammed into this seated block.]*

This week the holds reach their full length, and we keep the lengthening you learned last week so strong doesn't crowd out supple.

**Peak endurance holds.**

Lift small, hold for about **8 to 10 seconds**, breathing the whole way — then lower in control, never a drop. Rest as long as you held.

Let's do about **8**. At this length, the temptation is to grip harder and hold your breath. Don't. Keep the lift small; if you can't breathe through it, it's too big — shorten the hold.

*[~8 reps: 8–10s holds, controlled descent, rest ≈ hold length]*

A round of **hold-and-pulse** to keep the combo sharp — steady base, a few pulses on top, slow down. About **3**.

*[~3 hold-and-pulse reps]*

And a quick **supple check** — don't lose last week's work. A few gentle lengthens: in-breath, let the floor lengthen and open; out-breath, neutral. **Gentle, no force.** Just **3 or 4**, to keep the door open in both directions.

*[~3–4 gentle lengthening reps; reuse the "gentle only" guardrail from Week 6]*

**Now, the part that goes into your day.** This is the real point this week:

As you walk somewhere today — even across a room — keep the floor *gently* engaged for a stretch, breathing normally, then let it go. On stairs, a light engagement on the way up. Standing up from a chair, the knack you already know. Not braced and clenched — just *present*, then released.

### Reflect — one prompt
**Out in your day — could you keep the muscle gently engaged while walking, or did it slip the moment you moved?**
- Stayed with me

- Forgot to try — *(then: no problem, the cue gets more automatic with a few goes)*
