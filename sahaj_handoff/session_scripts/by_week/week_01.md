# Sahaj — Session Scripts
## Week 1: Foundation (Awareness)

> Convention for this file:
> **Plain text** = copy shown/spoken to the user.
> *Italic in brackets* = implementation note for Claude Code (timers, pacing, behaviour). Not shown to the user.

---

### Week 1 goal
Learn to *find* the pelvic floor, lift it correctly without dragging in the wrong muscles, and — most of all — fully release it. No strengthening this week. This is about sensation and control, not effort.

---

### Before you start — *show once, Week 1 Session 1 only*

A few honest ground rules:

- **Small and gentle wins.** A harder squeeze is not a better one. A pelvic floor that's always clenched causes more trouble than a weak one — so we train the *release* as carefully as the lift.
- **It should never hurt.** If anything here causes pain, stop. Pain isn't progress in this work; it's a sign to ease off, or to check with a doctor.
- **The "stop your pee" idea is only for finding the muscles.** Try it once to locate them, then don't make a habit of stopping your flow mid-stream — it's a locator, not an exercise.
- **Every session can be done sitting still, fully dressed, breathing normally.** No one around you would notice. That's by design.

---

### Session structure — *every session, all 12 weeks*
1. **Settle** (~2 min) — breathing, let tension go.
2. **Core work** (~6 min) — the week's technique.
3. **Down-regulate** (~2 min) — release fully, end calm.
4. **Reflect** — one prompt.

---

## Session 1 — full script

### Block 1 · Settle · 2:00
*[timer 2:00. Cadence cue underneath: inhale ~4s, exhale ~6s — longer out than in. Lines reveal slowly, unhurried.]*

Sit however you're comfortable — a chair, the edge of a bed. Let your shoulders drop.

Rest one hand on your belly.

Breathe in slowly through your nose, and let the belly rise into your hand — not the chest.

Now let it out, slow and long, like a quiet sigh.

Again. In, the belly rises... out, longer than the in.

Keep going at your own pace. With each out-breath, let a little more go — your jaw, your shoulders, the area between your sit bones.

### Block 2 · Core work · 6:00 — Finding the muscles
*[timer 6:00. Session 1 is spacious — mostly breathing, only a handful of gentle cycles with long rests. No rep counter shown.]*

We're going to find the pelvic floor. Two ways to locate it:

Imagine you badly need to pass gas and have to hold it in — that small internal lift.

Or the muscles you'd use to stop yourself peeing midstream. (Only to find them — don't actually do that while peeing as a habit.)

That gentle draw up and in — that's the muscle.

Now the important part: keep it *small*. We're learning the feeling, not lifting anything heavy.

And keep breathing. If you catch yourself holding your breath, you're trying too hard.

Quick check — rest your other hand on your belly or your buttock. They should stay soft. Only the floor inside works. Not the stomach, not the glutes, not the thighs.

Let's try a few, slow and easy.

On your next out-breath — lift, just a little.

And on the in-breath — let it go. All the way. Completely soft.

Feel that difference? Lifted... and fully let go. The letting-go is half the skill — maybe the more important half.

Again, no rush. Out-breath, gentle lift... in-breath, full release.

*[continue ~5–6 unhurried cycles, generous rest between each]*

If you can barely feel anything yet, that's completely normal. It's week one. The feeling gets clearer quickly.

### Block 3 · Down-regulate · 2:00
*[timer 2:00.]*

Stop lifting now. Let everything down there go heavy and soft — more relaxed than when you started.

A few more slow breaths. Long out-breaths.

Nothing to do here, nothing to achieve. Just sitting, breathing, calm.

That's the session.

### Reflect — one prompt
**Could you tell the difference between holding and fully letting go?**
- Yes, clearly
- A little
- Not yet — and that's fine

## Sessions 2–6 — deltas from Session 1

Same three-block shape; **Settle** and **Down-regulate** are unchanged. Only **Core work** moves:

- **Session 2** — Repeat Session 1's finding work exactly. Locating takes a few goes; today it just needs to feel more familiar than yesterday. Reflect prompt unchanged.

- **Session 3** — First gentle *holds*. Lift on the out-breath, hold softly for **2 seconds** while still breathing, then release on the in-breath and rest **at least 4 seconds** — rest longer than you held. ~6 reps. Cue line: "the rest matters more than the hold."

- **Session 4** — As Session 3, ~8 reps. Add the check: if the belly or glutes tense to help, that's too much — make the lift smaller, not bigger.

- **Session 5** — Holds of **3 seconds**, rest 5–6 seconds, ~8 reps. Reflect prompt changes to: **"Was the release as easy as the lift?"** *(The answer should be yes. If the release feels harder than the lift, that's the thing worth noticing.)*

- **Session 6** — As Session 5, ~10 reps. Closing line for the week: acknowledge — quietly, no fanfare — that they've just spent a week learning the foundation most men never learn, on their own terms.
