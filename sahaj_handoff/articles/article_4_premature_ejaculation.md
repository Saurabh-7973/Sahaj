## Premature ejaculation: common, and more workable than you think

Finishing sooner than you'd like is one of the most common sexual concerns men have — and one of the least talked about, which leaves most men assuming they're uniquely broken. They're not. It's ordinary, it's understood, and it responds to some surprisingly simple, low-cost approaches.

### What it actually is
The International Society for Sexual Medicine defines lifelong premature ejaculation, roughly, as consistently finishing within about a minute of penetration, with little sense of control over it, and with real distress as a result. There's also *acquired* PE — when it starts later, having not been an issue before. The distinction matters, because acquired PE can have an underlying cause worth checking. (Worth a small note of local pride: the expert panel that wrote these international guidelines met in Bengaluru.)

The key word in the definition is *distress*. Occasionally finishing fast is normal and not a disorder. PE is when it's persistent and it's bothering you.

### The good news: the evidence-based options are mostly cheap and drug-free
This is a field full of expensive miracle "cures" sold to anxious men. Most are nonsense. What actually has evidence behind it is, encouragingly, often free:

- **Behavioural techniques.** The two classics are the **stop–start** method (stimulate to near the point of no return, stop, let the urge fade, repeat) and the **squeeze** technique. Both train the same underlying skill: learning to recognise the mid-level of arousal — the point before the point of no return — and ease off it. Over time, the window widens.
- **Pelvic-floor training.** There's real trial evidence that pelvic-floor rehabilitation improves ejaculatory control, and studies suggest combining it with the behavioural techniques works *better* than the behavioural techniques alone. This is exactly what Sahaj builds — the control you're training is directly relevant here.
- **Medical options**, through a doctor, when they're warranted — certain medications and topical anaesthetics — alone or combined with the behavioural work.

### How this connects to the rest
Notice the theme running through everything: control, calm, and getting out of your own head. The same pelvic-floor control, the same down-regulation, the same presence that help erections and performance anxiety are the tools here too. PE often travels with performance anxiety, and easing one tends to ease the other.

### Caveats
- If it's **new** — you didn't used to have this problem and now you do — see a doctor, because acquired PE sometimes points to something treatable underneath (an erection issue, a prostate or thyroid matter). *(See: warning signs.)*
- Please don't spend money on unproven "stamina" pills or quack cures. The things that work are mostly skills, not products.
- This is one evidence-based piece, not a guaranteed fix for everyone; a doctor or sex therapist can help if self-guided work isn't enough.
