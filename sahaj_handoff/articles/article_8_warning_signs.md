## Warning signs: when it's a doctor's job, not a training one

---

This app can help with a lot. It would be dishonest to pretend it helps with everything. Some signals your body sends are not about training harder or being more patient — they're about getting seen by a doctor. Knowing the difference isn't anxiety; it's looking after yourself properly.

None of what follows is a diagnosis. It's a short, conservative list of "worth a doctor's eyes" — and the honest rule underneath it is: **when in doubt, get checked.**

### See a doctor before relying on training alone
- **Erection difficulty that came on suddenly** rather than gradually — especially if you have heart disease, diabetes, or high blood pressure, or you're over 40 with those risks. As the erections article explains, this can be an early warning about your heart, and it's worth acting on for far more than your sex life.
- **Blood in your urine or semen.**
- **A lump, swelling, or pain in the testicles.**
- **Persistent pain** in the pelvis, genitals, or the area between the scrotum and anus.
- **Pain or new difficulty passing urine**, or a marked change in your stream.
- **Symptoms that started after an injury, surgery, or a new medication.**

These don't mean something is necessarily wrong. They mean a doctor — your own GP or any clinician — should take a look before you assume it's something to simply practise your way through.

### Get urgent care now
A couple of things shouldn't wait:
- **An erection that won't go down, or has lasted for hours and is painful.** This can be a genuine medical emergency.
- **Numbness in the genital or "saddle" area, or new weakness or numbness in the legs.**

For these, contact a doctor or emergency services straight away — in India, **112**.

### And one more, just as important
If what you're carrying is heavier in your mind than your body — if you're feeling hopeless, or having thoughts of harming yourself — please reach out. You're not alone, and help is free and available any time:
- **TeleMANAS:** 14416 or 1-800-891-4416
- **KIRAN:** 1800-599-0019

### The honest takeaway
Training, sleep, and lifestyle do a great deal for your sexual health — but they aren't the answer to everything, and some things need a professional. Seeing a doctor is the strong move, not the failure. *(Helpline numbers and the specifics above should be re-confirmed by a clinician and kept current.)*
