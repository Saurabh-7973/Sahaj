## Delayed ejaculation: the quieter struggle

Premature ejaculation gets all the attention. Its opposite — taking a very long time to finish, or not being able to at all, especially with a partner — is just as real, just as frustrating, and even less talked about. If this is you, you've probably assumed something is deeply wrong. Usually, there's a clear and fixable reason.

### The cause most men never suspect: medication
This is the single most useful thing in this article. A very common cause of delayed ejaculation is **medication** — particularly certain antidepressants (the SSRIs), but also some blood-pressure medicines and antipsychotics. They're doing their job elsewhere and quietly dampening the ejaculation reflex as a side effect.

If your difficulty started after beginning a new medicine, that's likely your answer — and it's often solvable by adjusting the dose or switching to an alternative with fewer sexual side effects. **But never stop or change a prescribed medication on your own.** Talk to the doctor who prescribed it; this is a normal conversation for them.

### The other usual suspects
- **Psychological:** stress, low mood, anxiety, performance pressure, relationship strain — the mind can hold the brakes on.
- **Physical / neurological:** diabetes, nerve issues, past pelvic or prostate surgery, and naturally with age (sensation and testosterone shift).
- **Hormonal:** low testosterone, among others.
- **Heavy alcohol**, regularly.
- **Conditioning.** A telling sign: if you can climax easily on your own but not with a partner, the cause is usually psychological or a matter of conditioning — sometimes a very specific, idiosyncratic self-stimulation style the body has learned to expect, which partnered sex doesn't replicate. That's re-trainable.

### What helps
Treatment follows the cause: reviewing medications with your doctor, treating low testosterone if that's the issue, and counselling or sex therapy for the psychological and conditioning side — gently lowering the anxiety and, where relevant, broadening what the body responds to. As with everything in this app, calm and reduced pressure do a lot of the work.

### Caveats
- An occasional long night is normal. **Persistent** delayed ejaculation that bothers you is worth a doctor's visit — especially to review any medications you're on. *(See: warning signs.)*
- Skip the "performance" supplements; they don't fix this.
